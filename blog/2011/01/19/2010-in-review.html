The stats helper monkeys at WordPress.com mulled over how this blog did in 2010, and here's a high level summary of its overall blog health.

The Blog-Health-o-Meter™ reads This blog is on fire!.

Crunchy numbers

About 3 million people visit the Taj Mahal every year. This blog was viewed about 31,000 times in 2010. If it were the Taj Mahal, it would take about 4 days for that many people to see it.

In 2010, there were 8 new posts, growing the total archive of this blog to 80 posts. There were 13 pictures uploaded, taking up a total of 960kb. That's about a picture per month.

The busiest day of the year was April 21st with 163 views. The most popular post that day was Asus kEeeboard -- way kewl.

Where did they come from?

The top referring sites in 2010 were google.com, tikalk.com, google.co.in, blogs.sun.com, and stumbleupon.com.

Some visitors came searching, mostly for dunnet walkthrough, dunnet, eeekeyboard, emacs dunnet, and linux monospace font.
